## What This Question Catches

### ‚úÖ **PROVEN/DOCUMENTED Issues Only:**

| Category | What It Catches | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Visa Denials** | Any previous U.S. visa denial (B-2, F-1, H-1B, etc.) | Creates scrutiny, may indicate inadmissibility |
| **Entry Denials** | Turned away at U.S. border/airport | Indicates serious issue (fraud, misrep, criminal) |
| **Visa Cancellations** | Visa revoked by consular officer or CBP | Usually due to fraud or eligibility change |
| **Withdrawals** | User withdrew visa application | May indicate problem they wanted to avoid |
| **Deportations/Removals** | Formal removal by immigration judge | Creates 5-20 year bars (or permanent) |
| **Removal Proceedings** | Currently in or previously in proceedings | May have existing bar or pending case |
| **Overstays** | Stayed beyond I-94 authorized date | Creates 3-year bar (180-364 days) or 10-year bar (365+ days) |

### ‚ùå **What We Excluded (Unproven/Voluntary):**

- Lived without legal status (no way to verify unless caught)
- Worked without authorization (no way to verify unless caught)
- Provided false information (if caught, would result in denial or deportation above)
- Used fraudulent documents (if caught, would result in denial or deportation above)
- Entered without inspection (if caught, would result in deportation above)

**Rationale:** If unproven violations resulted in consequences, they'd show up as denials/deportations. If they didn't result in consequences, USCIS might not know about them, and users might lie on a voluntary disclosure question.
